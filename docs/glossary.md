# Topic 1 - Introduction to Cybersecurity: Glossary

**Last Updated**: July 02, 2026

---

## A

**AI-Powered Attacks**  
Cyberattacks that leverage artificial intelligence and machine learning to automate reconnaissance, evade detection, or craft more convincing phishing emails.

**Attack Surface**  
The total sum of all possible entry points, vulnerabilities, and attack vectors through which an unauthorized user could gain access to a system, network, or data.

**Attack Vector**  
The specific pathway, method, or technique that a threat actor uses to gain unauthorized access to a target system (e.g., phishing email, exploited vulnerability, malicious USB drive).

**Authenticity**  
One of the principles in the Parkerian Hexad. Refers to the assurance that a piece of data, message, or transaction is genuine and originated from its claimed source.

**Availability**  
One of the three pillars of the CIA Triad. Ensures that systems, data, and services are accessible and operational when authorized users need them.

---

## B

**Business World Context**  
Understanding that cybersecurity is not just a technical issue but a business risk management function that affects revenue, reputation, regulatory compliance, and customer trust.

---

## C

**Career Opportunities**  
The growing demand for cybersecurity professionals across multiple industries, including roles like SOC Analyst, Penetration Tester, Security Consultant, GRC Specialist, and Security Engineer.

**CIA Triad**  
The foundational security model consisting of three core principles: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.

**Common Threats**  
The most frequently encountered cyberattacks including malware, ransomware, phishing, DDoS attacks, supply chain attacks, and insider threats.

**Confidentiality**  
One of the three pillars of the CIA Triad. Ensures that sensitive data is only accessible to authorized individuals.

**Control (Possession/Control)**  
One of the principles in the Parkerian Hexad. Refers to the ownership and control over data.

**Core Concepts**  
The essential mental framework needed to understand cybersecurity, including the CIA Triad, risk management, and the threat landscape.

**Current Threat Trends (2026)**  
The evolving threat landscape including AI-powered attacks, ransomware-as-a-service, state-sponsored cyber warfare, supply chain compromises, and IoT/OT attacks.

**Cybersecurity**  
The practice of protecting systems, networks, programs, and data from digital attacks, damage, or unauthorized access.

**Cybersecurity vs Information Security vs IT Security**  
- **Cybersecurity**: Focuses on protecting digital systems and networks.  
- **Information Security**: Broader protection of all information assets.  
- **IT Security**: Focuses specifically on securing IT infrastructure.

---

## D

**DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)**  
A type of attack where multiple compromised systems flood a target with traffic to make it unavailable.

**Defense-in-Depth**  
A security strategy that employs multiple layers of defense so that if one layer is breached, others remain effective.

**Definition of Cybersecurity**  
The practice of protecting systems, networks, programs, and data from digital attacks.

**Digital Attacks**  
Any malicious attempt to compromise, damage, or gain unauthorized access to digital systems.

---

## E

**Exploit**  
A piece of code or technique that takes advantage of a vulnerability.

**Exploitation**  
The act of using an exploit to compromise a system.

---

## G

**Global Threat Landscape**  
The worldwide pattern of cyber threats, attacks, and adversaries.

**Growth of Cybersecurity Market**  
The rapid expansion of the cybersecurity industry driven by digitalization and rising threats.

---

## H

**High-Level Responsibilities**  
Strategic duties including risk assessment, security architecture, incident response, and compliance.

---

## I

**Impact**  
The potential damage or consequence resulting from a successful attack.

**Information Security**  
The practice of protecting all information assets (digital and physical).

**Infrastructure Targeting**  
Attacks aimed at critical infrastructure such as power grids and transportation systems.

**Insider Threats**  
Security risks originating from within the organization (employees, contractors).

**Integrity**  
One of the three pillars of the CIA Triad. Ensures data is accurate and has not been tampered with.

**IT Security**  
The discipline focused on securing IT infrastructure (networks, servers, endpoints).

---

## L

**Landscape (Cybersecurity)**  
The overall view of threats, vulnerabilities, actors, and defense mechanisms.

**Least Privilege**  
Granting users and systems only the minimum access necessary.

---

## M

**Malware**  
Malicious software designed to harm or exploit systems.

**Market Demand**  
The high and growing need for cybersecurity professionals and solutions.

**Mental Framework**  
The conceptual understanding needed to think about security effectively.

**Mobile Money Fraud**  
Fraud targeting mobile money platforms, especially relevant in Africa.

---

## N

**Networks**  
Interconnected systems that enable communication and data exchange.

**New Cyber Threats**  
Emerging attack methods such as AI-generated attacks and ransomware evolution.

---

## P

**Parkerian Hexad**  
An extended security model that adds Possession/Control, Authenticity, and Utility to the CIA Triad.

**Payload**  
The actual malicious component within malware or an attack.

**Phishing**  
Deceptive communications designed to trick users into revealing information.

**Possession/Control**  
Ownership and control over information assets.

**Programs**  
Software applications that run on hardware.

**Protecting Systems, Networks, Programs, and Data**  
The core scope of cybersecurity.

---

## R

**Ransomware**  
Malware that encrypts files and demands payment for decryption.

**Ransomware Evolution**  
Progression from simple encryption to double and triple extortion models.

**Realistic Expectations**  
Understanding the challenges and continuous learning required in cybersecurity.

**Risk**  
The combination of Threat × Vulnerability × Impact.

**Risk Management**  
The process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks.

---

## S

**Scope of Cybersecurity**  
The full breadth of what cybersecurity covers.

**Social Engineering**  
Psychological manipulation to gain confidential information.

**State-Sponsored Threats**  
Cyberattacks conducted by or on behalf of nation-states.

**Supply Chain Attack**  
Attacking a vendor or partner to breach the main target.

**System Protection**  
Securing computer systems and infrastructure.

---

## T

**Telecom Attacks**  
Attacks targeting telecommunications infrastructure (relevant in Africa).

**Threat**  
Any entity or action that has the potential to cause harm.

**Threat Landscape**  
The overall picture of current and emerging cyber threats.

---

## U

**Utility**  
One of the Parkerian Hexad principles — the usefulness of data.

---

## V

**Vulnerability**  
A weakness in a system that can be exploited.

---

## Z

**Zero Trust**  
Security model based on "never trust, always verify".

**Zero-Day**  
A vulnerability unknown to the vendor with no patch available.
